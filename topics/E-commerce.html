Age of E-Commerce

In the 1990s a new model of commerce emerged, which was based on using the Internet and harnessing the power of individual customers. New businesses were founded like Amazon.com and eBay.com, which operated their retail business fully online. Let us have a look at their operations.

Amazon.com

Jeff Bezos launched Amazon.com in 1995. His idea came from that time when he was working at a Wall Street firm. He observed the tremendous possibilities in using the Internet to sell products. He made a list of possible items to sell, and his final decision was: books. He reasoned that there were millions of book titles out in the market but there was not such a bookstore that could hold all these titles.

eBay.com

Pierre Omidyar thought a marketplace on the Web would be fairer and more accessible than any other existing market. So, he created eBay.com during a long weekend all by himself in 1995; he needed to code mostly three things for the future users of his auction site: how to create an item, a possibility for accessing all items others already created, and a feature to bid on these items.

The Use of the Internet Spreads Rapidly

Just to put into perspective how fast the Internet spread; to reach 50 million users:

  • Radio needed 38 years
  • Television reached this point in 13 years
  • The Internet had the same amounts of users only in 4 years
This explosive growth was driven by two facts. Firstly, we need to mention Moore’s law which says that the power of integrated circuits doubles every 18 months. Thanks to this fact, computers became smaller and less expensive, in a word, more accessible to an average customer. Secondly, according to Metcalf’s law, every new user does not just add a new connection to the network, its effect is much bigger, as it has the potential to connect to all the other users in the network. It means that as the number of internet users grows, the usefulness of the Internet grows too at a much higher rate.
